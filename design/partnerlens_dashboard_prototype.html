Modeling notes

  • Unit of analysis: the seller-order pair, not the raw order. Olist's order_items table allows more than one seller per order, so aggregating straight from orders would misattribute shared-order signals (delivery time, review score) to sellers who didn't drive them. Every feature below is built by first exploding to seller-order pairs, then aggregating up to seller level.
  • Monetary features: log1p(revenue) prior to scaling — raw revenue is heavily right-skewed by a small number of high-GMV sellers, which would otherwise dominate distance-based clustering.
  • Recency window: computed against the dataset's maximum order date (2018-10-17), not today() — this is a historical snapshot, not a live feed.
  • Known assumption — multi-seller review attribution: Olist review scores are attached to the order, not the line item, so an order with items from two sellers assigns the same review to both. This inflates negative-review rate for sellers who happen to co-occur with a poor performer. Quantify the share of affected orders directly from order_items.groupby('order_id').seller_id.nunique() and report it here — do not ship a placeholder number.
